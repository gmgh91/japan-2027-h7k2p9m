Leg 01 · 4 nights

Tokyo

Tue 2 — Sat 6 Feb
Land late, ease in. A buffer for jetlag, body clocks, and the toddler. Big-city anchor experiences before we head into the mountains.

Where we could stay

MIMARU Tokyo Asakusa Station — apt-hotel, family suites with full kitchens, lift access. 3 min walk to Asakusa Station. Property →TBC
Tue 2
TravelBrisbane → Tokyo Narita, lands 17:30. Transfer to hotel (~90 min). Conbini / hotel dinner — likely settled by 21:00.
Wed 3
AnchorMorning: Kappabashi-dori kitchen town (knives, ceramics, plastic food samples). Afternoon: Setsubun at Sensō-ji — bean-throwing festival, oni costumes.
Thu 4
AnchorMorning: Tsukiji Outer Market — sashimi breakfast, market wander. Afternoon: Hama-rikyu Gardens + tea house on the pond island. Gray + Sophie evening: Golden Gai tiny-bar crawl (Shinjuku) + whisky at Bar Zoetrope.
Fri 5
AnchorSumo morning practice (6:30am, adult split-cohort, Ryōgoku). Afternoon: depachika browse (Mitsukoshi / Isetan). Early night before Hakone.

Bonus — if you've got time

StationeryItoya Ginza — 13 floors of pens, washi, art supplies. Tokyo's stationery temple.
KidsPokemon Cafe + Pokemon Center DX (Nihonbashi) — Pokemon HQ. Cafe needs ~1 month advance booking.
ChaosDon Quijote (Akihabara or Shibuya) — multi-floor discount megastore, snacks, weird stuff, tax-free.
ArchitectureSumida Hokusai Museum — Kazuyo Sejima building + Hokusai prints. Pairs with Tsukiji morning.
WhiskyBar Zoetrope (Shinjuku) — 300+ Japanese whiskies, adult evening.
NightlifeGolden Gai (Shinjuku) — six tiny alleys of 4–8 seat bars, adults only.
Hands-onBuaisou indigo workshop (Kuramae) — traditional aizome dyeing, take a tote home.
Leg 02 · 2 nights

Hakone

Sat 6 — Mon 8 Feb
Onsen town. Mt Fuji views, volcanic valleys, and the slow-down day before Kyoto.

Where we could stay

Hakone Ashinoko Hanaori — modern lakeside ryokan with private bookable onsens (tattoo-safe), Western-Japanese hybrid rooms (beds + chairs), lift access, family suites. Property →TBC
Sat 6
TravelTokyo → Hakone on Odakyu Romancecar (~85 min). Lunch in Yumoto. Afternoon: Hakone Open-Air Museum. Check in to ryokan, onsen + kaiseki dinner.
Sun 7
AnchorThe Hakone Loop — Tozan switchback railway → cable car → ropeway over Owakudani (volcanic valley, black eggs) → pirate ship across Lake Ashi (Fuji views) → Hakone Shrine (torii in the water) → bus back.

Bonus — if you've got time

ArtPola Museum of Art — Impressionists in a forest setting, walk-able from the ropeway.
PhotogenicHakone Glass Forest (Venetian Glass Museum) — kid-friendly, very pretty.
Leg 03 · 6 nights

Kyoto

Mon 8 — Sun 14 Feb
The cultural heart and the depth leg. Eight nights to slow right down — temples, machiya streets, sake breweries, master craft. Wide day-trip range: Himeji, Nara, Fushimi sake, Yamazaki distillery, Osaka half-day, Kaiyukan aquarium.

Where we could stay

Ruikou Machiya (Machiya Residence Inn) — 4-bedroom heritage machiya, 184m², two buildings around a garden. Western beds + kitchen + dining for Mum and Dad in the detached hanare. Two kitchens. Property →Enquiry in
Backup 1: Miyagawacho Samurai Machiya (Gion-adjacent, 4–5 BR with multiple Western beds). Property →
Backup 2: Tofukuji Samurai Machiya (5 BR / sleeps 9, 2 Western beds + full kitchen, bookable now — no booking-window wait). Property →
Mon 8
TravelHakone → Kyoto via shinkansen (~2 hr 15). Check in.
Tue 9
AnchorHigashiyama / Gion day — Kiyomizu-dera, Sannenzaka/Ninenzaka, Yasaka Shrine, Gion wander. Optional free walking tour in the morning.
Wed 10
AnchorFushimi day — Inari shrine 1000 torii morning, Fushimi sake district afternoon (Gekkeikan museum + tasting, Kizakura).
Thu 11
AnchorMorning: Nara half-day — Tōdai-ji giant Buddha + deer park. Afternoon back in Kyoto.
Fri 12
AnchorFamily: Himeji Castle day trip (~50 min shinkansen). Keep climb + Koko-en gardens. Split-cohort if Goldie skips the keep. Gray + Sophie: depart Kyoto for Naoshima Island (~2.5 hr), Benesse House Museum + Kusama pumpkin afternoon, overnight on island.
Sat 13
AnchorMorning: forward bags to Nozawa via Yamato Takkyubin (arrives Sun at the lodge). Family: Kids day — Toei Kyoto Studio Park + Kyoto Railway Museum. Gray + Sophie: Chichu Art Museum + Lee Ufan Museum morning on Naoshima, ferry + train back to Kyoto by evening. Family dinner together.

Bonus — if you've got time

FoodNishiki Market — "Kyoto's kitchen," covered food street, snack as you walk.
NightlifePontocho alley — narrow lantern-lit alley packed with restaurants and bars after dark.
FoodKichi Kichi omurice — theatrical chef-as-show omurice (the famous YouTube one). Book ahead.
Calendar giftPlum blossom at Kitano Tenmangu — ~1,500 plum trees in peak bloom mid-Feb. Plum tea house in the grounds.
CultureTea ceremony with a master — proper sit-down, ~90 min. Operators: Camellia, En, Hozan-ji.
KimonoKimono dress-up in Gion — get dressed and photographed in old Kyoto streets.
BambooArashiyama half-day — bamboo grove + Tenryū-ji + Togetsukyo bridge. Skip the monkey park.
TempleSaiho-ji Moss Temple — advance postal reservation only, sutra-copying meditation, otherworldly.
